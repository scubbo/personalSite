One of the most famously beautiful results in mathematics is Euler's Identity. This can be expressed as

\[ e^{iπ} + 1 = 0 \]

This page is an attempt to explain both the beauty of the result, and the beauty of its derivation.

The intention of this is to demonstrate that all mathematical discoveries build on lower-level results or discoveries. In this way, we can use a short sentence to express a very complicated concept, because each word or phrase can be "unpacked" to give more meaning. Thus, with no "drilling down", the proof of the identity is simply as follows:

Note to self - definitions of e, cos, and sin here :)

\[ e^{ix} = \sum\limits_{n=0}^{∞} \frac{x^n}{n!} \]

Huh? Where did that come from?

This is called a "Taylor Series"

\[ e^{ix} = cos(x) + i sin(x) \]

\[ \implies e^{iπ} = cos(π) + i sin(π) = -1 + 0 = -1 \]

\[ \implies e^{iπ} + 1 = 0 \]
